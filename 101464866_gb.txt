What is George Brown College?

George Brown College also known as The George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology
is a public college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. The College was established in 1867
during the formation of Ontario's community college system, it is named after George Brown
founder of The Globe and Mail and one of the Fathers of Confederation. George Brown currently has
3 campuses (Casa Loma, St. James and Waterfront) and a plethora of diploma and degree programs to
choose from. In the recent years George Brown has also created online degrees and diplomas, so 
that students can pursue their education virtually no matter where they are. George Brown has
many notable alumni like Mark McEwan, John Henry and Rob Rainford.
